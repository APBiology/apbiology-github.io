‘Random occurrences that effect evolution’ and the H-W Equilibrium

 

Evolution works in largely different ways, but sometimes, it is just random. We cannot really predict if all of us are going to die tomorrow, and even if we did, we might be able to defend ourselves against the natural forces of the universe.

 

Let us take the example: One day, a natural disaster kills off most people on Earth, except those few people who were sent to space, for example. The space shuttle has 5 males and females each. Those 10 people will be the representations and the last of us, humanity. Now, the genetic traits they have do not represent everyone that lived before the disaster - that is simply not possible. We can conclude that the gene pool decreased drastically in diversity. Now, they are living in a space station that is floating outside the Earth's atmosphere. If they reproduce and have offsprings, their genes will be very limited, compared to before the disaster. In fact, so much people died that maybe most of the gene variety is gone. We can call this the bottleneck effect. Actually, the founders effect also works in this case. Now, let me explain the 2.

 

Bottleneck effect: Imagine a small rabbit population were faced against a massive twister. Most died due to destruction of sustainable homes, and also hit by flying objects. Let's say 10 out of a 100000 survived. Their genes, which by complete chance may have had the e allele for red eyes (for example) instead of the more common (before disaster) E allele for green eyes. Now, this survival may or may not be due to natural selection based on fitness, but often more purely by chance of survival. But due to this, this specific species of organisms will survive and have only red eyes.

 

Founder's effect: This is very similar to the Bottleneck effect but really is about becoming a founder. Like the founding father s of America, let's say the rabbits moved to a far far away island by some means of transport. They is no other rabbit of this psecies and they are the pioneers, thus, founders. By random chance, the rabbits that moved might have only had red eyes instead of green eyes, even if green was the more common color. Their offsprings will do so too, and this pattern may cause speciation with distinct traits.

 

Random mutations: One of the most common change, or evolution are random mutations. Mutations can be caused just by the UV lights from the sunlight. Sometimes, they may knock out a some base pairs and we may not notice it. But there are cases where a beneficial mutation is received, and there is also equal chance of malicious mutations, like that of cancer. It is completely random and sometimes this allows so much variation that when a virus or pathogen in general wipes over the population, the variety allows immunity of some species. That is why most scientists say that variety displays the success of a species.
