Here is an interview with Edward. Let's see what he has to say about what he learned about evolution throughout history and the advent of early life forms.

 

1. How has life evolved throughout history?

 

Edward:

Life evolved through history sometimes slowly, sometimes quickly.  Its generally agreed that all organisms evolved from a single prokaryote, and it got its life-necessary components and macromolecules from the environmental conditions existing during the beginning years of Earth.  Life evolved after unicellular organisms began engulfing other unicellular organisms, or asexual reproduction gone wrong, and multicellular organisms began to arise too.  The organisms which reproduce more can pass down their genetic information more, so those with similar genes that helps the organism survive and reproduce with pass that information down.  Evolution is usually affected by the genes which help the species stay alive and reproduce, so the characteristics of species usually depend on the environmental factors at those times.

 

2. How does speciation and isolation work? Talk about reproductive isolation, physical isolation.

 

Edward:

Speciation usually occurs when there is some sort of barrier between populations.  Whether it be geographical, mechanical, or behavioral, if the sperm is not able to reach the egg or if the zygote can not stay healthy or fertile, after more and more generations of these occurrences, there will be a point when the two populations are no longer able to reproduce.
